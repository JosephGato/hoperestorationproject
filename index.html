Our Challenge is Poverty

Poverty comes in all forms, physical, spiritual, emotional, relational, economic.
It robs people of their basic needs— food, clean water, sanitation, healthcare, and
education. It destroys hope, purpose, and self-worth. It infects hearts with isolation,
apathy, dependency, disconnection, and addiction. Poverty is life fallen
short of God’s intent. As our vision grows, we’re expanding to
rural communities that are predominantly Muslim or
traditional African cult religions to share the hope of the Gospel.
Following our vision and mission, we aim to initiate partnerships with
local organizations on the ground and involve the communities
at all stages of the project cycle
